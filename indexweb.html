Roots run deep.

Three close friends who grew up dancing together.

We're Elena, Rashmika, and Kiarra, and honestly we're just big dance enthusiasts. We love all of it (classical, Indian dance, hip-hop), and we're still learning and growing with every class we take and teach. We started Rhythm & Roots, a youth-led dance collective, to share that love with our community and put it behind something that actually matters.

Here's the part that means the most to us: we don't keep any of it. Everything we earn goes to Kranti, an organization that empowers and trains vulnerable young women in India so they can become leaders and change their own communities.

Honestly, we have a little too much fun doing this. That's kind of the whole point.

All roots, all rhythm, all heart.

It's the only way we know

We think dance belongs to everyone.

It's joyful, it's a little bit powerful, and it pulls together people who might never have crossed paths otherwise. So our floor is open to everyone, wherever you're from, whatever you believe, whether you've danced your whole life or never once in your kitchen.

Good to know

A few details before you join.

Who can join?
Our Kids Semi-Classical series is built for ages 6 to 10. Bollywood in the Park is open to everyone, so bring the whole family. Our performance troupe is for middle and high schoolers.
What should we wear and bring?
Come in comfy clothes you can really move in, plus sneakers and a water bottle. Please skip the jeans, they make it tough to dance.
Can we just drop in?
For Bollywood in the Park, yes, come whenever you like. For the kids' six-session series, it's best to start from the first class so nobody misses a step.
Do dancers under 18 need a parent there?
Yes. Anyone under 18 needs a parent or guardian to sign them in before class.
How do we book a party or performance?
Just text or call any of us, our numbers are right down below, and we'll figure out the date, the songs, and the rest together.
What happens if a class is cancelled?
If we ever have to cancel, we'll reschedule it. And if you have a question about a payment, just reach out, we're easy to talk to.

Why we do this

Helping young women in India write their own story.

Every dollar we raise goes to Kranti, an organization that gives vulnerable young women real education, support, and leadership training so they can lead change in their own communities. Dance and movement are a big part of how they heal and find their voice.

That's the part that gets us. The same dancing that fills our floor with joy is helping these girls rise, and that feels like the best reason to keep going.
